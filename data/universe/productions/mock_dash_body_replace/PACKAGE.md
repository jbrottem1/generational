## Full Script

Right now, your body is quietly replacing itself. Not all at once — piece by piece. Your skin cells renew on a short cycle, shedding the old and building the new. Red blood cells last about four months… then fresh ones take the job. Different organs refresh on different clocks. You're less a fixed object — and more a living construction site. So the 'you' watching this is already becoming the next you.

## Scientific Notes
- Many tissues renew on different timescales (e.g., skin epidermis days–weeks; red blood cells ~120 days).
- Avoid overprecise viral claims like 'new body every 7 years' as absolute — frame as continuous replacement of many parts at different rates.
- Use simplified organ icons, not medical gore.
