## Full Script

The ocean is secretly glowing — and most of that light is alive. Down here, sunlight barely reaches… so life makes its own. Tiny plankton flash like blue sparks when the water moves. Jellies and deep-sea fish use chemical light — a reaction that turns energy into a glow. Some use it to hunt. Some use it to escape. Some use it to talk. It's called bioluminescence — living light. So next time you hear the ocean is dark… remember: it's also a night sky underwater.

## Scientific Notes
- Bioluminescence = light produced by living organisms via chemical reactions (often luciferin + luciferase).
- Common in marine environments; functions include predation, defense, communication.
- Keep claims general; avoid overstating specific species behavior without naming them carefully.
