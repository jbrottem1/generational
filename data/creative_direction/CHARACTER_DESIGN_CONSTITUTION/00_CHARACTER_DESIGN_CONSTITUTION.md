# Generational Character Design Constitution

**Phase:** XII  
**Status:** Permanent studio law for every Generational character  
**Universe:** Hopeful Educational Cinema (`GENERATIONAL_UNIVERSE/`)  
**Architecture:** Frozen · rules evolve identity — not engines  
**Scope:** Humans · clinicians · teachers · children · patients · animals · robots / androids  

**This document does not design a character.**  
It defines the rules that make characters timeless.

Companion: `01_PASS_FAIL_STANDARDS.md` · Audit: `02_DOCTOR_001_CONSTITUTION_AUDIT.md`

---

## 0. Preamble

Every Generational character must feel like they were designed by one studio for one emotional contract:

> Curiosity · Trust · Hope · Discovery · Intelligence · Compassion · Wonder

Not cartoon slapstick. Not clinical coldness. Not science-fiction menace.

A character that fails these laws may be technically animated — it is still **not Generational**.

---

## 1. Character philosophy

### Law
Characters are **companions in learning**, not mascots bolted onto content.

### Principles
1. Appeal before dialogue  
2. Trust before cleverness  
3. Clarity of feeling over micro-detail  
4. Vocational truth (role readable in silhouette when relevant)  
5. Belonging to the same universe as every other character  
6. Designed for twenty years of recognition, not one viral tick  

### Forbidden philosophies
Threat-as-cool · smug genius · blank industrial tool · interchangeable white robot · uncanny “real person” default · joke body as educational host  

### Pass / fail (philosophy)
| ID | Standard | Pass |
|----|----------|------|
| P-01 | Mute still communicates welcome or curiosity | Required |
| P-02 | Role does not depend on text labels alone | Required |
| P-03 | Fits Hopeful Educational Cinema medium definition | Required |

## 2. Shape language

### Law
Soft forms create trust; clean forms create intelligence; open curves create discovery.

| Intent | Forms |
|--------|-------|
| Trust | Soft rounds, gentle ovals |
| Intelligence | Clean verticals, calm rectangles |
| Discovery | Soft arcs, open curves |
| Tech | Rounded rectangles |

### Forbid
Aggressive spikes · military wedges · toy-perfect sphere stacks as hero body language · chaotic bolt clouds  

### Pass / fail
| ID | Standard | Pass |
|----|----------|------|
| S-01 | Dominant forms map to trust/intelligence/discovery table | Required |
| S-02 | No banned threat geometry on hero characters | Required |
| S-03 | Shape language matches other Generational characters in same shot | Required |

---

## 3. Facial construction

### Law
Faces are continuous forms that carry kindness before speech — not stickers on a ball.

### Requirements
- Clear head volume with intentional cranial silhouette  
- Forehead / brow shelf capable of thought  
- Cheeks as soft volumes (not balloon orbs)  
- Jaw transition into neck (no floating head)  
- Mouth plane with lip volumes that smile and speak  
- Micro-asymmetry at rest allowed (2–5%)  

### Pass / fail
| ID | Standard | Pass |
|----|----------|------|
| F-01 | Features feel integrated into head sculpt | Required |
| F-02 | Neck–head transition is continuous | Required |
| F-03 | Smile and speech coexist without destroying lip shape | Required |
| F-04 | Resting soft warmth present at low intensity | Required |

---

## 4. Eye hierarchy

### Law
Eyes are the emotional anchor. Pupil hierarchy beats glow.

### Stack (required order of readability)
1. Dark decisive pupil  
2. Readable iris (teal family when brand-android)  
3. Soft sclera  
4. Soft lid volumes  
5. Small specular highlight  
6. Near-zero emissive (no headlights)  

### Pass / fail
| ID | Standard | Pass |
|----|----------|------|
| E-01 | Pupil darker / clearer than iris in CU | Required |
| E-02 | Lids close believably in blink | Required |
| E-03 | No headlight / laser emissive as default | Required |
| E-04 | Gaze convergence readable toward learner or evidence | Required |

---

## 5. Mouth hierarchy

### Law
Mouth teaches speech and emotion; it is never a painted slash alone.

### Stack
1. Upper / lower lip volumes  
2. Dark soft cavity cue when open  
3. Corners capable of up/down  
4. Viseme shapes change aperture (not jaw-only)  
5. Rest soft smile surviving CU  

### Pass / fail
| ID | Standard | Pass |
|----|----------|------|
| M-01 | Distinct lip volumes visible in CU | Required |
| M-02 | Emotion readable mute (smile / concern / neutral) | Required |
| M-03 | Speech shapes change mouth silhouette | Required |

## 6. Silhouette rules

### Law
If shadow fails, the design fails.

### Requirements
- Recognizable at ~20 m equivalent / mural crop  
- Recognizable in pure black  
- Recognizable without facial detail  
- One signature silhouette idea (not a pile of accents)  

### Pass / fail
| ID | Standard | Pass |
|----|----------|------|
| SIL-01 | Blackout still: stranger names role or character | Required |
| SIL-02 | Blackout distinct from generic white humanoid pile | Required |
| SIL-03 | Signature silhouette element present without color | Required |

## 7. Proportion system

### Law
Proportions serve educational cinema appeal — not MetaHuman realism, not chibi cartoon.

### Guidelines
| Type | Guide |
|------|-------|
| Adult educator / Doctor | ~6–7.5 heads; slight head overscale (~1.06–1.10) OK for CU |
| Child | Larger head relative; soft limbs; never cruel caricature |
| Soft android mentor | Approachable heroic height; coat/torso volume may dominate if garment-identity |
| Forbid | Extreme stilts · balloon toddler proportions on adult hosts · uncanny long necks |

### Pass / fail
| ID | Standard | Pass |
|----|----------|------|
| PR-01 | Proportions match character role typology | Required |
| PR-02 | Hands readable for teaching gestures | Required |
| PR-03 | Not intimidating scale toward children in shared frames | Required |

## 8. Gesture philosophy

### Law
Gesture invites learning. Default is open, never closed judgment.

### Signature vocabulary (hosts)
Open-palm invitation · point-of-care to evidence · soft presenting lift · listening tilt  

### Forbid
Crossed arms as default · finger-wag shame · weaponized grip · vacant T-pose as brand imagery  

### Pass / fail
| ID | Standard | Pass |
|----|----------|------|
| G-01 | Default teaching gesture is open / inviting | Required |
| G-02 | Hands support evidence visibility | Required |
| G-03 | Gesture readable at mid-shot | Required |

## 9. Clothing philosophy

### Law
Cloth is character. Vocation should be readable as garment, not decals on a nude shell.

### Requirements
Fabric truth · soft folds or intentional volume · role silhouettes (coat, cardigan, scrubs, kid clothes) · brand marks rare and precious · movement of cloth as secondary animation when relevant  

### Pass / fail
| ID | Standard | Pass |
|----|----------|------|
| C-01 | Clothing reads as garment in blackout | Required for clothed roles |
| C-02 | Brand trim is accent, not slab noise | Required |
| C-03 | Cloth does not scream toy plastic identical to shell | Required |

## 16. Black silhouette testing

### Procedure
Render or draw pure black silhouette — front, ¾, teaching pose.  
Five reviewers answer: Who is this?  

| ID | Standard | Pass |
|----|----------|------|
| BL-01 | ≥4/5 reviewers give correct character or correct iconic role | Required |
| BL-02 | Zero reviewers say only “a robot / a person” with no specificity | Required |

## 17. Motion personality

### Law
Motion continues character — same personality in walks and stills.

Require: anticipation · follow-through · ease · breathing · eye-leads-head · weight · secondary cloth when clothed  

Forbid: robotic holds · instant pose snaps · image-plate Ken Burns as “performance”  

| ID | Standard | Pass |
|----|----------|------|
| MO-01 | Walk / idle reads same personality as still appeal | Required |
| MO-02 | Cloth or soft secondary motion present when garment is identity | Required for coat-identity roles |
| MO-03 | No dead holds longer than pedagogical hold justifies | Required |
