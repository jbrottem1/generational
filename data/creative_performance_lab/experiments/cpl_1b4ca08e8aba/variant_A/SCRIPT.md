# Why Octopuses Have Three Hearts

**Variant:** A
**Hook style:** curiosity_gap
**Hook:** You've seen an octopus move — but almost nobody asks why its blood system is built like this.

## Full script

You've seen an octopus move — but almost nobody asks why its blood system is built like this. An octopus has three hearts. Two push blood through the gills. The third pumps blood to the rest of the body. When it swims, the systemic heart nearly stops — which is why octopuses prefer crawling. Blue copper blood and distributed intelligence make this animal even stranger. Follow for the next underwater myth we'll unpack.
