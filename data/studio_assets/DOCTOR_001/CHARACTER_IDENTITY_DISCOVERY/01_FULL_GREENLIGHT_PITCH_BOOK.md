## One-second silhouette law

If a child sees only blackout for one second and cannot say **“That’s The Doctor,”** the concept has not earned permanent identity.

Current prototype fails this law.  
This book proposes five replacements for the *idea*, not polish of the robot.

# CONCEPT A — The Living Lab Coat ★

### Pitch sheets
| Sheet | Path |
|-------|------|
| Model turnaround (front / side / ¾ / face / silhouette) | `pitch_sheets/A_living_coat/01_model_turnaround.png` |
| Expressions + hero poses | `pitch_sheets/A_living_coat/02_expressions_poses.png` |
| Lab · teaching · learners · thumbnail | `pitch_sheets/A_living_coat/03_context_thumbnail.png` |

### Design philosophy
The coat **is** the character. Hospitality, medicine, and teaching made into garment silhouette. The android shell is the actor inside an iconic costume-body — never the logo.

### Visual language
Dawn-white fabric volumes · flared hem · stand collar · cuff breaks · teal pin as seed · soft mentor face as support · no chrome joints · soft curves + clean vertical coat edges.

### Emotional impact
Welcome before words. Soft authority. “Come learn with me.”

### Educational trust
Highest among five. Coat instantly signals clinician-educator to parents and institutions.

### Memorability
Child drawing: big white coat, teal pin, open hand. Not “white robot.”

### Animation potential
Strong coat follow-through, hem, sleeve gesture; face remains expressive; cloth discipline required.

### Merchandise potential
Exceptional — coat figures, pins, end-card icons, plush coat silhouette, mural cutouts.

### Strengths
Owns a niche Baymax/WALL-E/EVE do not. Passes mural blackout. Dual audience. Thumbnail: coat+pin+face still read.

### Weaknesses
Cloth simulation / garment sculpt hard. Soft volumes can slide toward Baymax if undisciplined.

### Long-term risks
If execution stays sphere-torso-with-paint, A collapses back to current failure. Identity succeeds only if coat remains *fabric truth*.

### One-second answer
**“The Doctor with the big coat!”**

# The twenty-year question

> If Generational became a billion-view educational brand, which character would still feel timeless in twenty years?

## Choice (only one): **Concept A — The Living Lab Coat**

### Greenlight defense (Pixar / Disney / DreamWorks / Sony / Fortiche room)

You asked for iconic — not safe.

**D** is safer and tasteful. Vertical mentors age well because they resemble classical teacher statues. That is also why they fail the billion-view icon test: they share category space with every lecturer, every robe, every documentary host. Timeless *taste* is not the same as timeless *recognition*.

**B** and **E** are logo-clever. Crescent and ring will look brilliant on a pitch wall in 2026. In 2046 they risk reading as era-specific tech ornament — headsets, halos, wearable UI residue.

**C** is emotionally irresistible in CU and will win any shorts board. It is also the fastest path to “cute robot with big eyes,” a cemetery of forgotten educational mascots and WALL-E comparisons. Warmth without ownership is amnesia.

**A** alone converts a universal cultural object — the **medical teaching coat** — into silhouette destiny.

Baymax owns care-volume soft body.  
WALL-E owns lonely binoculars.  
EVE owns hovering ovoid purity.  
The Doctor must own **the living coat that invites learning**.

That icon scales:

- child drawing  
- grayscale parade balloon  
- muted TV in a hospital waiting room  
- university mural  
- merch pin on a parent’s bag  
- YouTube tile at 120 pixels  

It stays educationally trustworthy without needing cute inflation.  
It stays warm without becoming babyish.  
It stays merchandisable without becoming a headset fad.  
It survives twenty years because coats, teaching, and invitation are older than the internet — and Generational’s promise is lifelong learning, not seasonal tech fashion.

### Guardrails (non-negotiable if greenlit)

1. Coat must be **fabric garment silhouette**, not marshmallow fat.  
2. Hem flare + collar + cuff breaks required in every blackout.  
3. Head is supporting actor; coat is lead.  
4. No chrome joint balls.  
5. Teal pin is companion identity, not the silhouette.  
6. Fail any still that reads “white robot in a sweater.”

### What we refuse

- Fusion of A+B+C+D+E (returns generic)  
- Approving the current mesh as if it were A  
- Choosing D because it feels executive-safe

**DOCTOR_001 is the Living Lab Coat:**  
a warm medical android whose recognizable existence is a soft, flared teaching coat that invites learners in — with kind eyes and a Generational pin as companions to that silhouette, never substitutes for it.
