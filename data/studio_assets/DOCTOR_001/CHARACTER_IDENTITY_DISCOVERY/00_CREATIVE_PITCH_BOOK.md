# Phase IX — Creative Pitch Book  
## DOCTOR_001 · Iconic Identity Discovery

**Studio:** Character Design · Identity Discovery  
**Architecture:** Permanently frozen · **No implementation this phase**  
**Question:** If a child saw only the silhouette for one second — what single feature makes them say *“That’s The Doctor”*?

---

## Prologue — Why we stopped refining

Incremental sculpt improved intention. It did not create destiny.

The current Doctor still answers the one-second test with:

> “A white robot.”

Icons do not emerge from polishing assemblies.  
They emerge from **one ruthless visual idea** that owns blackout.

This book does not improve the prototype.  
It discovers who The Doctor **is**.

---

## The one-second law

Every direction below must answer:

**Core silhouette word** → child utterance

If a direction needs color, face detail, or story to be recognized, it fails Phase IX.

# Direction A — **The Living Lab Coat**

**Sketch:** `sketches/01_A_living_coat.png`

### Core visual concept
The coat **is** the character. Not cloth on a robot — a soft medical garment-body whose flared hem, stand collar, and teaching sleeve define the outline. The android shell exists *inside* the coat, barely competing.

### Signature silhouette
Flared medical coat trapezoid · soft collar V · small head peeking · one open teaching hand from a sleeve.

**One-second answer:** “The Doctor with the big coat!”

### Head shape
Small-to-medium soft egg, nestled in collar — **head never outranks coat.**

### Face design
Warm, simplified mentor face; secondary to silhouette.

### Eye design
Kind medium-large eyes; trust on CU, not primary silhouette.

### Coat philosophy
Garment-first. Collar, lapels, hem flare, sleeves as logo volumes. Teal pin as merchandise seed, not silhouette.

### Body proportions
Coat volume dominates torso; limbs readable but subordinate; soft shoulders under fabric.

### Signature pose
¾ welcome stance, coat hem settled, chest open to camera.

### Signature gesture
Open palm invitation emerging from sleeve cuff.

### Why people remember it
Owns what Baymax / WALL-E / EVE do **not**: *medical teaching coat as body*. Educational clarity at mural scale.

### Risks / weaknesses
- Soft volume can slide toward Baymax if coat becomes marshmallow blob  
- Hard to animate without cloth discipline  
- If coat fails, whole identity fails

# Recommendation — Permanent identity thesis

## Choose: **Direction A — The Living Lab Coat**

### The one-second answer (finally)

> Soft flared medical coat with open teaching hand.  
> **“That’s The Doctor.”**

### Why A beats the current Doctor

| Current | Direction A |
|---------|-------------|
| White rounded robot with accents | Coat *is* the figure |
| Silhouette = sphere stack | Silhouette = garment trapezoid |
| Memory = robot | Memory = Doctor’s coat |
| Competing with every white android | Owns teaching-coat niche vs Baymax/WALL-E/EVE |
| Pin/teal as stickers | Pin becomes merchandise of a silhouette that already won |

### Why A beats B–E for Generational

- **Educational trust:** Coat = medicine + classroom instantly (beats crescent, lantern, vertical-only).  
- **Silhouette law:** Works without face or color (beats pure optic).  
- **Merchandise:** Coat figure, pin, end-card, mural — coherent.  
- **Differentiation:** Does not borrow WALL-E eyes, Baymax puff, EVE ovoid-as-body.  
- **Brand truth:** Generational sells lifelong learning with care — the coat is hospitality in clothing form.

### Guardrails (so A does not become Baymax)

1. Coat must read as **fabric garment**, not marshmallow fat.  
2. Hem flare + collar V + sleeve cuff = required breaks.  
3. Head stays **supporting actor**; coat is lead.  
4. Teal pin is secondary lock, never primary silhouette.  
5. Soft navy may linger as cuff/cranial accent only — never chrome balls.

### What transfers from Concept C / Phase VII–VIII

Warmth, teal sparingly, dawn white, kindness-before-dialogue — **kept**.  
Sphere-stack body as identity — **discarded**.  
“Mentor with coat polish” — **upgraded to “coat is character.”**

# Permanent identity statement (proposed)

**DOCTOR_001 is the Living Lab Coat:**  
a warm medical android whose recognizable existence is a soft, flared teaching coat that invites learners in — with kind eyes and a Generational pin as companions, never substitutes, to that silhouette.
